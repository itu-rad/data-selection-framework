### Instruction:
Describe the structure of an atom.

### Response: 
An atom is the smallest unit of ordinary matter that makes up all matter, and it consists of three main parts: protons, neutrons, and electrons. 

The protons and neutrons are found in the nucleus, which is the center of the atom. The number of protons in the nucleus defines the element to which the atom belongs, and the number of neutrons can vary to create different isotopes of the same element. 

The electrons orbit around the nucleus. The number of electrons in an atom is equal to the number of protons, and this number determines the chemical properties of that element. The electrons are arranged in energy levels or shells, with each shell capable of holding a specific number of electrons. 

In summary, the structure of an atom consists of a nucleus containing protons, neutrons, and a shell of electrons that surrounds the nucleus and can hold a specific number of electrons.
